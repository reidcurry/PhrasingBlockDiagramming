# Mark Dever on Community Groups — Sermon Prep Summary

A brief research summary of how Mark Dever teaches about small groups, membership, and one-another ministry through 9Marks and Capitol Hill Baptist Church (Washington, DC). For sermon preparation—not a transcript or quote collection.

*Companion to [John Piper on Community Groups](john-piper-on-community-groups.md).*

---

## Why Dever Preaches About Community Groups

Dever treats **small groups as helpful but not essential**—a useful tool under pastoral oversight, not a ninth mark of the church or a substitute for the gathered congregation ([On Small Groups: Helpful, not Essential](https://www.9marks.org/episode/on-small-groups-helpful-not-essential/)).

Key motivations in his teaching:

- **Church first, groups second** — You can be a biblical Christian without a small group; you cannot without a local church ([Using Small Groups to Cultivate Fellowship](https://www.9marks.org/article/using-small-groups-cultivate-fellowship/)).
- **Meaningful membership** — Pastors must give account for souls (Heb. 13:17); groups extend care but membership defines who is under that care ([Twelve Steps to Regain Meaningful Membership](https://www.9marks.org/article/twelve-steps-to-regain-meaningful-membership/)).
- **Discipling in context** — Discipling is "helping others follow Jesus by doing deliberate spiritual good to them," and the **local church is the primary context** ([*Discipling*](https://www.crossway.org/books/discipling-tpb/)).
- **Gospel-revealing community** — Real fellowship crosses natural affinities and displays God's wisdom to the world ([*The Compelling Community*](https://www.9marks.org/books/the-compelling-community/)).
- **Thoroughfares, not cul-de-sacs** — Groups must push people into the wider church, not replace it ([Small Groups: Thoroughfares not Cul-De-Sacs](https://www.9marks.org/article/small-groups-thoroughfares-not-cul-de-sacs/)).

## Purposes of Small Groups (in Dever's Framework)

1. **Extend elder oversight** — Leaders facilitate pastoral care under elder authority; groups are "an extension of the elders' pastoral ministry" ([Using Small Groups to Cultivate Fellowship](https://www.9marks.org/article/using-small-groups-cultivate-fellowship/)).
2. **Foster individual discipleship** — Community is a by-product and means toward producing faithful Christians, not an end in itself.
3. **Apply congregational teaching** — Sermon reflection, Bible study, and prayer turn whole-church instruction into personal application.
4. **Connect congregation and one-on-one relationships** — Groups occupy the middle of a discipleship spectrum: whole church ↔ small groups ↔ one-on-one.
5. **Serve as thoroughfares** — Leaders should help members love and serve people *outside* the group ([Small Groups: Thoroughfares not Cul-De-Sacs](https://www.9marks.org/article/small-groups-thoroughfares-not-cul-de-sacs/)).
6. **Protect unity and safety** — Member-only church-sponsored groups enable honest conversation and pastoral care ([Mailbag #72](https://www.9marks.org/answer/mailbag-72-small-groups-with-christians-from-other-churches-which-churches-should-we-pray-for-publicly/)).

## What Happens in Groups (Capitol Hill / 9Marks Vision)

CHBC-style community groups (documented by Michael Lawrence, under Dever's eldership) emphasize:

- **Spiritual fellowship** — Not pure support groups or study clubs; fellowship informed by Scripture, prayer, and interpersonal reflection
- **Microcosm of the whole church** — "General community groups" reflect congregational diversity, not niche affinity
- **Pastor-approved content** — Leaders choose Bible, book, or sermon-based study with elder input
- **Open culture** — Willingness to receive new members; periodic reassignment to avoid cul-de-sacs
- **Discipling relationships** — Groups facilitate, but do not replace, one-on-one discipling
- **Member participation encouraged, not required** — Whole-church gatherings take priority when time is limited
- **Evangelistic groups separate** — Non-member outreach groups exist; ongoing discipleship groups are for members

He illustrates failure modes: groups that become relational destinations, static cliques proud of longevity, or substitutes for Sunday worship and membership commitment ([Should I Join a Church for Its Small Groups?](https://www.9marks.org/article/should-i-join-a-church-for-its-small-groups/)).

## Common Dever/9Marks Themes for Preaching

### 1. Helpful, not essential
Small groups are a wise ministry tool, not a biblical requirement for what constitutes a church ([On Small Groups: Helpful, not Essential](https://www.9marks.org/episode/on-small-groups-helpful-not-essential/)).

### 2. Meaningful membership
Membership is "everywhere implied" in the NT—discipline, submission to elders, and inside/outside distinctions require identifiable commitment ([Church Membership and Prescriptive Implication](https://www.9marks.org/article/church-membership-and-prescriptive-implication/)).

### 3. Church before group
Join for shared doctrine and the whole congregation, not for a comfortable sub-group. The church's statement of faith precedes relational affinity ([Should I Join a Church for Its Small Groups?](https://www.9marks.org/article/should-i-join-a-church-for-its-small-groups/)).

### 4. Discipling as deliberate spiritual good
Discipling is a relationship of teaching, correcting, modeling, and loving someone toward Christ—primarily in the local church ([*Discipling*](https://www.crossway.org/books/discipling-tpb/)).

### 5. Gospel-revealing vs. gospel-plus community
Supernatural community unites people who share little except Christ; affinity-based groups may thrive but do not display the gospel's power ([*The Compelling Community*](https://www.9marks.org/books/the-compelling-community/)).

### 6. Unity tool or unity threat
Small groups can foster covenant living or fracture the body; structure and leader training matter ([How Small Groups Can Foster Church Unity . . . Or Destroy It](https://www.9marks.org/article/how-small-groups-can-foster-church-unity-or-destroy-it/)).

### 7. Expositional listening
Healthy churches hear God's Word preached; groups apply what the congregation has heard together—a pattern consistent with expositional ministry (9 Marks framework).

## Capitol Hill Baptist Structure (Historical Context)

Under Dever's leadership, CHBC developed community groups that:

- Sit under elder oversight with quarterly leader training
- Require leaders to be members in good standing attending both Sunday gatherings
- Encourage (but do not mandate) group participation at membership interviews
- Limit most groups to members; offer separate evangelistic Mark studies for non-Christians
- Periodically add members and reassort groups to prevent isolation
- Prioritize whole-congregation teaching over group life when schedules conflict

This structure aims to make **Ephesians 4:11–12** and **Hebrews 13:17** operational: elders equip and give account; saints do the work of ministry in accountable community.

## Preaching Angles Informed by Dever

When expositing community-group passages in this repo, Dever's framework suggests emphasizing:

- **Membership is meaningful** — One-another commands assume identifiable commitment to a local church
- **Groups serve the congregation** — Application: Does this group send people deeper into church-wide life?
- **Discipling is intentional** — Relational ministry is deliberate spiritual good, not accidental proximity
- **Unity over comfort** — Diversity in groups mirrors the gospel; affinity cliques are a warning sign
- **Elders give account** — Structure exists so pastors can watch souls, not merely run programs
- **Love proves itself** — Compelling community is verified in sacrificial action (1 John 3:16–18)
