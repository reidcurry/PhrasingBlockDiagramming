# Acts 2:42–47 (ESV)

**Sermon angle:** The prototype community — devoted to the apostles' teaching, fellowship, the breaking of bread, and prayers.

## Text

> [42] And they devoted themselves to the apostles' teaching and the fellowship, to the breaking of bread and the prayers. [43] And awe came upon every soul, and many wonders and signs were being done through the apostles. [44] And all who believed were together and had all things in common. [45] And they were selling their possessions and belongings and distributing the proceeds to all, as any had need. [46] And day by day, attending the temple together and breaking bread in their homes, they received their food with glad and generous hearts, [47] praising God and having favor with all the people. And the Lord added to their number day by day those who were being saved.

## Notes

- **42** — Four devotions define community life: teaching, fellowship, table, prayer.
- **44–45** — Shared life expresses itself in generosity toward need.
- **46** — Large gathering (temple) and household gathering (breaking bread) together shape the rhythm.
- **47** — Worship and witness; the Lord grows the community.
