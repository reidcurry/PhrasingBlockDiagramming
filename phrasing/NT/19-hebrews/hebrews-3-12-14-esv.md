# Hebrews 3:12–14 (ESV)

**Sermon angle:** Guard against an unbelieving heart; exhort one another daily while it is still "today"; sharing in Christ depends on holding firm to the end.

## Text

> [12] Take care, brothers, lest there be in any of you an evil, unbelieving heart, leading you to fall away from the living God. [13] But exhort one another every day, as long as it is called "today," that none of you may be hardened by the deceitfulness of sin. [14] For we have come to share in Christ, if indeed we hold our original confidence firm to the end.

## Notes

- **12** — Individual danger: an evil, unbelieving heart that falls away from the living God (echoes the wilderness generation in 3:7–11).
- **13** — Communal remedy: daily mutual exhortation so sin's deceitfulness does not harden anyone. `as long as it is called "today"` echoes Psalm 95 (quoted in 3:7–11, 15): the present era of God's voice is still "today," which grounds why exhortation is *every day*.
- **14** — Theological ground: participation in Christ is real `if indeed` original confidence is held firm to the end.
