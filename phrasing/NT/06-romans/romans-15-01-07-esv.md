# Romans 15:1–7 (ESV)

**Sermon angle:** Strong bear with weak — please the neighbor for his good, following Christ's example, living in harmony that glorifies God together.

## Text

> [1] We who are strong have an obligation to bear with the failings of the weak, and not to please ourselves. [2] Let each of us please his neighbor for his good, to build him up. [3] For even Christ did not please himself, but as it is written, "The reproaches of those who reproached you fell on me." [4] For whatever was written in former days was written for our instruction, that through endurance and through the encouragement of the Scriptures we might have hope. [5] May the God of endurance and encouragement grant you to live in such harmony with one another, in accord with Christ Jesus, [6] that together you may with one voice glorify the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ. [7] Therefore welcome one another as Christ has welcomed you, for the glory of God.

## Notes

- **1–2** — Strength in community is measured by bearing with others, not self-pleasing.
- **3–4** — Christ and Scripture supply the pattern and hope for costly welcome.
- **5–7** — Harmony and mutual welcome aim at unified worship and God's glory.
