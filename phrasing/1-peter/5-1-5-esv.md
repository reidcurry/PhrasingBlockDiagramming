# 1 Peter 5:1–5 (ESV)

**Sermon angle:** Dever's eldership text — shepherds exercise willing oversight; members submit in humility; small-group leaders extend this pattern under elder authority.

## Text

> [1] So I exhort the elders among you, as a fellow elder and a witness of the sufferings of Christ, as well as a fellow partaker in the glory that is going to be revealed: [2] shepherd the flock of God that is among you, exercising oversight, not under compulsion, but willingly, as God would have you; not for shameful gain, but eagerly; [3] not domineering over those in your charge, but being examples to the flock. [4] And when the chief Shepherd appears, you will receive the unfading crown of glory. [5] Likewise, you who are younger, be subject to the elders. Clothe yourselves, all of you, with humility toward one another, for "God opposes the proud but gives grace to the humble."

## Notes

- **2–3** — Oversight is willing, eager, and exemplary — not coercive or mercenary.
- **4** — Future reward from the Chief Shepherd motivates faithful shepherding.
- **5** — Mutual humility governs the whole congregation, not only leaders.
