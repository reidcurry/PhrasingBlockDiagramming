# Ecclesiastes 4:9–12 (ESV)

**Sermon angle:** Better together — companionship brings reward, rescue, warmth, and strength that solitude cannot supply.

## Text

> [9] Two are better than one, because they have a good reward for their toil. [10] For if they fall, one will lift up his fellow. But woe to him who is alone when he falls and has not another to lift him up! [11] Again, if two lie together, they keep warm, but how can one keep warm alone? [12] And though a man might prevail against one who is alone, two will withstand him—a threefold cord is not quickly broken.


## Notes

- **9** — Thesis: partnership improves life's labor.
- **10–11** — Three illustrations — fall, cold, attack — expose the vulnerability of isolation.
- **12** — Escalation to "threefold cord" suggests strength beyond mere pairs (often applied to God-centered community).
